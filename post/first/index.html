Hello, world!

Hey, I’m Jēkabs - a high-school student from Riga State Gymnasium No. 1, Latvia. Academically, I’m interested in mathematics, computer science and economics. I particularly enjoy working on projects that require me to combine models and intuitions from at least two of these and many other fields (of which I know too little of to concretely list them). I’ve also always liked tinkering with tech, whether that’s Lego robots in 2016 , or home automation and computer networks now.

My main purpose for making this is to sporadically clear up and concretize my thoughts or what I’ve learned about various topics that interest me, because all too often I find myself in situations where an intuition that I’ve confidently held spontaneously falls apart when said (or attempted to be said) out loud. I suspect this is mainly because when a line of reasoning that aligns with my intuition is missing a piece, I often resort to trusting that that particular piece of information must exist somewhere in my mind even if I can’t really put a finger on it. Mentally, this heuristic would be impossible to live without, however, over-reliance on this type of intuition at best leads to an inability to properly collaborate and at worst - an inconsistent internal model of the world.

I don’t particularly enjoy either of these notions

…so I’ve started this blog.

What does this mean for the kinds of topics I may explore?

I do not know. I’m still in the early stages of figuring out what lies at the heart of my interests, so your guess is almost as good as mine :)
